Is there a surplus of programmers in 2020?

Posted on 2020-06-13 |

Seeing that programmers have little chance of interviewing without three years of experience?

What skills do you have that require more skill than the real job?

Even programmers who have been working for some years are embarrassed to be unable to answer questions in interviews?

More and more training institutions of all kinds?
Programmers at work need to study various new technologies or algorithms that are not commonly used at work after work, so that the underlying principles can remain competitive?

The recruitment was released and dozens or hundreds of resumes were received.

Does the above situation mean that the programmer is saturated and surplus?
If there is a surplus, what is the ratio of supply to demand?

Here is an answer:

Excess.
It is very simple to see whether an industry is surplus or not. You just need to see if your competitors are similar to you.

But whenever you find a large number of your competitors:

I am older than you, have higher education background, better technology, more experience, and require the same salary as you.

That is iron surplus.

Don’t listen to those who say that there is no surplus at the high end and surplus at the low end. Of course, the high end is scarce everywhere.

But as a human being, don’t think you are superior to others.
