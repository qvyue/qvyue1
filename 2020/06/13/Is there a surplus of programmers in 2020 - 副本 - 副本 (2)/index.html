Is there a surplus of programmers in 2020?

Posted on 2020-06-13 |

I am going to send this answer three times and return it to the draft box three times, because I expect it will lead to war … I don’t want to touch on this topic which will lead to war if I am not careful. But I brushed up a few answers that made me very uncomfortable. But let’s answer the question first:
At present, the general environment is seriously affected and all walks of life are basically having a hard time.
It is inevitable that programmers will face greater competitive pressure. Not only programmers are inevitable, but all professions will or have already experienced this process. Why? If a profession is in a state of short supply for a long time and the society does not increase the output of talents in this profession … it can only be a social problem.
Therefore, from the perspective of social development. Programmers will definitely have a relative surplus as the supply of talents increases. This is the fate that no profession can escape. There is no such thing as a son of heaven in this world.
As long as the development of social productive forces cannot catch up with the supply of talents, relative surplus is inevitable. But this year and in the years to come this situation … productivity is not bad until it is retired.

The division of labor in modern society means that any occupation is essential in the short term. Therefore, don’t belittle other industries by such small-child bickering tactics as how important our industry is. There is no other meaning except to annoy people.
To tell the truth, there are some answers that use “programmer a bug will destroy billions”, “trading system has a bug direct loss xx” to indirectly raise their own can be saved.
