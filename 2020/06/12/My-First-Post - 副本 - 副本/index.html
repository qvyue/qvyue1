Is there a surplus of programmers

Posted on 2020-06-12 |

Programmers are essentially different from workers in textile factories, shoe factories and steel factories who burn boilers.
Attention is paid to the essential difference in working methods, not to say that there are differences in class status as workers. In class status, there is no essential difference in the type of work as a programmer between workers in textile factories, shoe factories and steel factories who burn boilers.
The biggest difference between programmers and other types of labor is that programmers’ labor cannot be simply duplicated, while the labor of textile factory workers and shoe factory workers is basically simply duplicated, producing the same products day after day with the same technology and little difference in appearance.
Programmers are closer to jobs that cannot be simply copied by early blacksmiths, modern doctors, lawyers, etc., but programmers work directly in the abstract layer instead of the physical layer and do not need physical copying.
Ford invented assembly line production, which breaks down the process of manufacturing a car and trains workers into simple and duplicated labor force specialized in manually completing the process, thus improving labor productivity. However, in the software development industry, enterprises that want to refer to Ford’s assembly line approach have all failed, and none of them have succeeded, because computers can complete tasks that can be simply copied without manual copying. Even in modern automobile manufacturing, simple processes are completed by robots, and workers are no longer required to repeat simple labor manually.
Programmers are one step closer than craftsmen, doctors and lawyers. Therefore, I said that the type of work of programmers has undergone qualitative changes and is essentially different from other types of work. This qualitative change is that programmers will eliminate the work of programmers who once existed. For example, early programmers did not have a good debugger, and it was very important for them to understand stacks. Then programmers built advanced debuggers for themselves. Now programmers do not need to understand stacks of binary data at all. This kind of phenomenon has also happened to artisans, but artisans still need to repeatedly create their own tools to facilitate their own, but programmers do not need to. After a tool is invented, it can be shared by all programmers without the traditional “duplication” of labor costs. The speed of self-innovation is unprecedented in any traditional labor.
This rapid self-innovation has led programmers to be more like researchers and engineers than workers who provide simple repetitive work. Any simple skills already mastered quickly become outdated, and any project is like a brand-new research. This qualitative change will have a great impact on the composition of the labor force, and more and more labor will work at the abstract level instead of making physical copies through simple labor-assisted machines.
This qualitative change will also lead to a change in the class status of the working class. In the past, the ruling class could control the operation of the society by controlling all kinds of physical resources through ownership. However, if the whole society works at the abstract level, the concept of ownership of the ruling class will fall apart. When ordinary physical objects are no longer important, more important ideas, algorithms and ideas are difficult to label as ownership and can be quickly shared. The meaning of the traditional ruling class will be shaken.
In the future, everyone may be “programmers”, but probably not all of them need to write code.
